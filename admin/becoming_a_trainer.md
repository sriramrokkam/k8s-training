# How to become a trainer for Docker and Kubernetes Fundamentals
Whether you have participated in course already or just have broad K8s knowledge and a passion for teaching - we are always happy to onboard new trainers.

## Motivation
Typically, we schedule classes with 15 and 35 attendees. While the ppt presentations are a great helper to convey the concepts, the training greatly benefits from live demos accompanied by explanations.

In order to teach a class you should feel comfortable in the role of an instructor. Of course, it is perfectly fine to grow into such a role, but you should at least ask yourself a few questions beforehand:
- Do you enjoy sharing your knowledge with others?
- Do you feel comfortable standing in front an audience talking about technical content? 
- Do you have a passion for improvising and doing technical demos?

## Prerequisites
Even the most motivated trainer candidate needs some time to prepare and host a class. Hence, a vital prerequisite is the backing of your manager and a capacity/time committment. The course duration is 4 days, but with some preparation work it can easily consume 6-7 working days per training.

In addition you should already have a good understanding of:
- Docker
- Container Images and their distribution
- Kubernetes
    - Cluster architecture
    - Workload resources (pods, deployment, statefulset)
    - Networking resources (service, ingress)
    - Workload administration (namespaces, service accounts, rbac, ...)
- Helm
- Linux scripting

## Onboarding
New trainers are not expected to host a complete training all by themselves. Instead of, you start as a co-trainer with a more experienced instructor. 

Ideally, you team-up for multiple trainings so you can get some feedback and gradually take over more content. For your very first training, it would be expected of you to prepare one topic per day.
